The ESP32-C6 does not directly support ROS 1 as a native ROS node. It is better suited for micro-ROS, which belongs to ROS 2. In a ROS 1 setup, the practical solution is to let the ESP32-C6 send data over Wi-Fi using UDP/TCP or serial, and then use a ROS 1 node on the Linux PC to translate that data into ROS topics.
